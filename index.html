The most commonly used cake ingredients include flour, sugar, eggs, butter or oil or margarine, a liquid, and a leaving agent, such as baking soda or baking power. Common additional ingradients and flavourings include dried, candied, or fresh fruit, nuts, cocoa, and extracts such as vanilla, with numerous substitutions for the primary ingredients. our speciality

Butter Cakes

Any recipe for cake that begins "cream butter and sugar" is a butter cake. flour to give it structure and texture, and baking power or baking soda to ensure that it rises in the oven.

Pound Cakes

Pound cake is a relative of butter cake. it's so called because it can be measured as a matter of proportion: a pound of butter, a pound of sugar, a pound of eggs, and a pound of flour.

Sponge Cakes

Any recipe that contains no baking soda or baking powder but lots of whipped eggs or egg whites? That's a sponge cake and there are several different types of sponge cakes.

Genoise Cakes

In Italy and France, a sponge cake is called genoise; in genoise, whole eggs are beaten with sugar until they're thick and ribbony, and then flour is added and better is baked.

Biscuit Cakes

Biscuit cakes are another type of sponge cake containing both egg whites and yolks, but, unlike genoise, the whites and yolks are whipped separately and then folded back together.

Angel Food Cakes

Angel food cakes are made with egg whites alone and no yolks. The whites are whipped with sugar until very firm before the flour is gently folded in, delicate with fruit.

Chiffon Cakes

This fairly recent American creation was inverted by a salesman who sold the recipe to General Mills, which spread the recipe through marketing materials in the 1940s and 1950s.

Carrot Cakes

Carrot Cake uses the leavening practices of butter cake, but instead of butter uses a neutral oil like vegetable or canola oil.
